01 · the new term

Dead ball, then crank σ

Ball at home, at rest. Rung 1 says: both terms zero, nothing happens, forever. The new term breaks that truce — every frame, a small random shove with no reason at all. σ (sigma) is its size. Start at zero. Crank it slowly.

a = -k * (x - home) - c * v // the old friends, unchanged v += a * dt + σ * √dt * gauss() // ← the new ingredient x += v * dt

The violet flicker is each frame's random shove; magenta is the spring answering it. Neither wins — the ball wanders around home, never repeating, never leaving. This object has a name (Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process); the name matters zero. Fine print, one line: the kick is scaled by √dt so the feel doesn't change with framerate — random kicks add up like a drunkard's walk, by the square root.

02 · randomness needs a home

Kill the spring, keep the noise

Left: random kicks only — k = 0, no home. Right: same kicks, same σ, spring on. Watch the left one long enough and it ends up anywhere; randomness without a home drifts forever (it wraps to stay on stage). The spring turns aimless drift into a texture that stays where you put it.

The readouts count distance from home. Left grows without bound — a random walk. Right hovers. Every "idle breathing" texture you've ever liked is the right panel; every janky drifting particle bug is the left one.

05 · the crowd — and the crack in the wall

Forty wanderers, one flaw

A field of wanderers, identical knobs. Toggle who shares the dice.

Independent: boiling static — every blade of grass in its own private storm. Shared: the whole field moves as one rigid sheet — a photograph on a shaky table. Neither is wind. Wind is the in-between: neighbors move similarly, far apart moves differently. That needs randomness that lives in space — a noise field, sampled by position. That's rung 3. This page can't do it. That's the cliff.
