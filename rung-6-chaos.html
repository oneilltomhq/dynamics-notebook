01 · three lines

The whole animal

Here is the complete system. No gauss(), no Math.random(), no hidden table. Three state variables, three lines, one knob: ρ. Same loop as always — state makes force, force remakes state — just fed back on itself.

x' = 10·(y − x) y' = x·(ρ − z) − y z' = x·y − (8/3)·z // that's it. that's the whole animal.
/lorenz/ρ28.0

Drag ρ slowly from 0 to 45 and watch the label. The knob doesn't change the amount of motion, it changes the kind: sink, then two still points, then spirals that eventually give in, then — past ρ ≈ 24.7 — motion that never gives in at all. Regime boundaries are cliffs, not slopes. You met regime-thinking in rung 2's knob grid; here it's sharp. This is why param intuition is the language: one number moves the system between universes.

02 · the butterfly, measured

Twins, one part in a million apart

Two copies of the recipe at ρ = 28. Identical in every way, except the cyan one starts 0.000001 to the side in x. If determinism meant predictability they would shadow each other forever. Watch the gap readout, and the strip below — it plots their separation on a log scale.

The straight climb on the log chart means the error doubles on a schedule — no measurement, however fine, buys you more than a little extra forecast time. Try the 1e-12 restart: a million times more precise, and it only postpones the split by a few seconds of the same straight line. This is why weather has a horizon. Determinism and predictability come apart: you can know the recipe perfectly and still not know next month.

03 · two of your pendulums, chained

The fine print comes due

Rung 1 left you a promise: "the real pendulum's pull is sin(x); that gap is where chaos sneaks in — double pendulum is two of yours chained." Here it is. Same masses, same rods, nothing but gravity and the chain. The magenta trail follows the second bob.

Rung 1's pendulum with the sin() fine print, squared. Drop it low and it's two springs — predictable, gently periodic, rung 1 with extra steps. Swing it high and the sin() nonlinearity takes over: same object, other side of a regime cliff. The tiny twin is section 02 again, but made of hardware you already own — offset by a ten-thousandth of a radian, it matches for a while, then lives a different life.

04 · chaos or dice?

Name that texture

Rung 2's game, final round. Two dots wander. One is rung 2's OU wander — a random number generator rolls dice every frame. The other is a Lorenz projection, scaled and slowed: pure clockwork, zero dice. They are not labeled. Watch as long as you like, then commit.

One has a random number generator in it; one is pure clockwork. If you can't tell, that's the point. Two different roads to aliveness: dice (rung 2) and folding (this rung) — stretch the state space, fold it back, repeat forever. The fluid from rung 5 uses the second road too: turbulence is chaos, not noise.
