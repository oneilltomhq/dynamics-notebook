01 · the hill problem

Same landscape, two marching orders

Rung 3 gave every point in space a number — a smooth landscape ψ(x, y, t), painted here as green haze; bright is high. The obvious move is to push particles with its slope. Here is why that fails, and the fix, side by side. Both halves show the identical field. Left follows the slope. Right follows the slope turned a quarter turn.

g = [ ψ(x+ε, y) − ψ(x−ε, y), // the slope — finite difference, ψ(x, y+ε) − ψ(x, y−ε) ] / 2ε // four samples, nothing fancy flow = [ g.y, −g.x ] // ← rotate 90°. the entire trick. v = flow · strength
/field/scale8
/flow/strength140

The left flow has sinks — places where more arrives than leaves. That imbalance has a name, divergence, and any field with nonzero divergence will bunch particles somewhere. The rotated field provably has none: what flows into any region flows back out, always, everywhere. Fluid people say incompressible. Watch the right half as long as you like — it circulates forever and never thins or piles.

02 · the rotation, visible

Watch every arrow turn in place

Strip the particles away and look at the raw arrows. The haze is the same kind of landscape, posterized into bands so you can read its contour lines. Toggle between the two fields and watch each arrow pivot 90° where it stands — same lengths, same anchors, one rotation.

Gradient arrows point uphill — across the bands, toward bright. Curl arrows are the contour lines come alive: they run along the band edges. Flow along a level set never changes the level, so nothing ever accumulates anywhere. That single sentence is the whole mathematics of this page.

03 · smoke

Three thousand particles, zero physics

Now spend it. Particles ride the curl field, the field itself drifts through time (ψ has a third axis — remember rung 3), and each frame fades instead of clearing, so motion leaves ink. That is the entire recipe. Let it run.

No fluid solver anywhere on this page. No pressure, no grid, no memory — the field doesn't even know the particles exist. That ignorance is the cost: real smoke pushes back, curls around obstacles, remembers where it's been; this smoke can't. That's rung 5.

04 · costume check

Three rungs, one lineup

Identical particles, three aliveness recipes from three rungs. Left: rung 2 — every particle rolls its own dice around a home. Middle: rung 3 — pushed by the raw field's slope. Right: rung 4 — pushed by the rotated slope. One energy knob feeds all three.

You should now be able to name each texture on sight. Boiling static with no travel: jitter — that's σ·√dt·gauss(). Slow herding into knots: drift-and-clump — that's v = g. Endless circulation, evenly spread: swirl — that's v = [g.y, −g.x]. Param → texture, both directions. One line of code each.
