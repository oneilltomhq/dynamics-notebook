dynamics notebook · rung 17 — general relativity

Curvature tells the planet where to fall.

Rung 16 built curvature from a twisted arrow. Rung 8 built orbits from a 1/d² pull. Bolt them together and you get Einstein's picture: a planet in free fall is just walking the straightest possible line — a geodesic — through spacetime the star has curved. That one change to Newton's law makes orbits slowly turn, bends starlight, and carves a point of no return. Same three lines; the pull now has a relativistic tail.

01 · the ellipse that won't close

Newton draws an ellipse. Einstein draws a rose.

Both planets start with the same position and speed and feel almost the same pull. The only difference is a tiny extra term in Einstein's law — u″ + u = M/L² + 3M u² versus Newton's bare u″ + u = M/L² (with u = 1/r). Newton's orbit closes into a fixed ellipse forever. Einstein's perihelion creeps forward every lap, tracing a rosette. This is the anomaly in Mercury's orbit that first told us Newton was incomplete.

/orbit/perihelion r14 M

The faint cyan ellipse has the same near and far points as the live orbit — it is the shape Newton would trace and keep forever. Einstein's orbit fills the same lobe but swings its perihelion forward each lap. Slide the perihelion inward for a closer, more relativistic pass and the rose spins faster; push it out and Einstein settles back toward the fixed ellipse. The whole effect is the tiny 3M u² term — nothing for planets, everything near a black hole.

02 · the hill that runs out of wall

Newton's cliff always turns you back — Einstein's doesn't

Reduce the orbit to a marble rolling in an effective potential of the radius alone. Newton's centrifugal wall +L²/2r² rises forever as r→0, so any infalling body swings back out. Einstein adds −ML²/r³, which eventually overpowers the wall and opens a cliff to r = 0. Cross the hump and there is no coming back — the marble falls in.

/orbit/angular mom L4.60
/marble/energy E−0.28

The dip is a stable circular orbit; the hump is an unstable one. Push L below √12 M and the dip and hump merge and vanish — the last stable orbit, the ISCO at r = 6M, the inner edge of every accretion disk. Newton has no such edge; his wall never quits.

03 · even light has to fall

Straight rays, bent around a star

Fire a comb of parallel light rays past the mass. Each one follows its own straightest path through the curved geometry and swings toward the star — the closer the pass, the sharper the bend, by ≈ 4M/b, exactly twice what a naïve "light is a fast bullet" guess gives. On the far side the rays cross: the star has acted as a lens. Eddington measured this in 1919 and made Einstein famous overnight.

/star/mass M1.2

The bright knot where the rays converge is a focus; line the source, lens, and eye up perfectly and the focus smears into an Einstein ring. The same equation that precesses Mercury and swallows light at the horizon is bending these rays — one geometry, every scale.
