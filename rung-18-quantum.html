dynamics notebook · rung 18 — the quantum wave

The state is a complex wave.

Every rung so far tracked real numbers — a position, a field value. Quantum mechanics keeps a complex one at every point: ψ = a length and a phase, exactly rung 7's little rotating clock, now spread over space. Its evolution is the same shape as rung 14's field, with one fateful factor of i: i ∂ψ/∂t = −½ ∂²ψ/∂x² + V ψ. That i turns spreading into interference, and the whole strange catalogue — tunneling, quantized levels, zero-point jitter — falls out of it.

Re ψ — the real part Im ψ — the imaginary part |ψ|² — the probability you can measure

01 · a particle is a moving lump of phase

Watch the real and imaginary parts wind around each other

Here is a free electron: a wavepacket gliding right. The amber and cyan curves are Re ψ and Im ψ — the two spin around each other as the phase turns, faster where the momentum is higher. The green envelope, |ψ|², is all a detector ever sees: the probability of finding the particle there. Notice it spreads as it moves — the same dispersion as rung 14's massive field, because a free particle is one.

/packet/momentum k0.60

More momentum k = tighter phase winding = faster glide. A sharp packet (well-known position) is built from many momenta and spreads fast; a broad one holds together — the uncertainty principle, visible as a trade between how localized the green lump is and how fast it smears.

02 · through a wall it hasn't the energy to climb

Part of it leaks out the other side

Send the packet at a barrier taller than its energy. Classically: total reflection, full stop. Quantum mechanically the wave doesn't stop dead at the wall — it decays inside and, if the wall is thin enough, a sliver emerges on the far side and keeps going. That leaked lump is a real particle with real probability. Raise the barrier and the leak shrinks; lower it below the packet's energy and most of it sails through (with a ghostly reflected piece — even then it isn't all-or-nothing).

/barrier/height V₀0.34

The transmitted and reflected fractions are printed live. This leak is not a metaphor: it is how the sun fuses, how alpha particles escape nuclei, how a scanning-tunneling microscope reads single atoms. A wall is only ever probably a wall.

03 · rung 1, quantized

Drop the wave into a spring well and it sloshes like the ball

Put the electron in a harmonic well — rung 1's potential, ½kx² — displaced from the bottom. Its probability lump swings back and forth at exactly the classical frequency ω, its center tracing ⟨x⟩ = A cos ωt, the very trajectory of rung 1's spring (Ehrenfest's theorem). But it can never sit perfectly still at the bottom: the ground state keeps a residual jitter, the zero-point energy ½ℏω. The ladder of allowed energies is drawn at the side.

The classical ball (amber dot below) and the quantum center-of-probability move in lockstep — the bridge from this rung all the way back to the first. Quantize the spring and you have the photon, the phonon, every particle in every field: they are all this one well, counted in rungs of ℏω.

And that phase at every point? Multiply ψ everywhere by the same e^{iα} and nothing measurable changes — a global U(1) symmetry, rung 7's shared clock. Demand the freedom to choose α differently at each point and, to keep the physics, you must invent a connection to compensate: the electromagnetic potential of rung 15. That is where the next rung goes.
