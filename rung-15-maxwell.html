dynamics notebook · rung 15 — maxwell

Two fields, each the other's cause.

Rung 14 was one field feeding itself. Maxwell's leap: use two. A changing electric field builds a magnetic one; a changing magnetic field builds an electric one. Wire that mutual feedback into a grid and light falls out for free — a self-sustaining ripple that needs no medium and moves at exactly one speed, c. This is the second piece of Witten's paragraph, the gauge field, in its oldest and friendliest form.

01 · light, made on a lattice

Poke the vacuum — rings race out at c

Click anywhere to drop a spark of electric field. It doesn't just sit: its change spins up a magnetic ring, whose change rebuilds the electric field a step further out, and the pulse walks itself across the grid at the one speed the two update rules allow. Bounce it off the metal walls. Flip on the antenna to drive it forever.

Nothing in here knows what "a wave" is. Each cell only runs two lines: add up the curl of its neighbor field, step. The circular wavefront, the reflections, the interference where reflected rings cross — all emergent. This grid is a literal Yee lattice, the workhorse of every antenna and photonics simulator on earth.

02 · the same sloshing as the spring

E-energy and B-energy trade places, forever

Seal a 1-D box with mirrors and ring it. The electric field (amber) and magnetic field (cyan) are a quarter-cycle out of step in time: when E is fully stretched across the box, B is flat, and an instant later B is peaked while E has vanished. Watch the two energy bars below — one empties exactly as the other fills. This is rung 1's potential-energy ↔ kinetic-energy sloshing, wearing Maxwell's clothes.

// Faraday: a changing E twists up B B[i] += dt * (E[i+1] - E[i]); // ∂B/∂t = −∂E/∂x (curl) // Ampère: a changing B twists up E E[i] += dt * (B[i] - B[i-1]); // ∂E/∂t = −∂B/∂x (curl)

Two lines, mirror images, each reading the other's slope. Energy ½E² + ½B² is conserved (the tiny drift is the grid rounding). Kill one line and the box goes dead — light exists only because both fields refuse to stop feeding each other.

03 · the field shows its hand

One wave, two slits, a striped screen

A plane wave marches in from the left and hits a conductor with two gaps. Each gap becomes a fresh source; the two spreading fronts overlap and, where crest meets crest, they reinforce — where crest meets trough, they cancel. The time-averaged brightness collected at the right edge is the interference pattern. Nothing particle-like is happening: it is two solutions of the same field equation, added.

/slits/separation28

The bright bars on the right are wide with one slit, finely striped with two — the signature of a wave, not a hail of pellets. Rung 18 will hand this exact striped screen to a single electron, and the stripes will still be there.

One rung deeper hides underneath all of this: E and B are shadows of a single object, the potential A, through E = −∂A/∂t − ∇φ, B = ∇×A. You can shift A in ways that leave E and B untouched — a gauge freedom. Promote that freedom from a curiosity to a law and you get Yang–Mills, the road to rung 20 and the Standard Model.
